The History of Matcha

Origin

Matcha originated in China during the Tang Dyanasty (7th-10th century). It became more commonly used during the Song dynasty as tea leaves turned into bricks for trade. A Japanese buddhist monk named Eisei brought the tea leaves back to Japan along with the Zen Buddhist way of preparing powdered tea to consume. During the Kamakura shogunate, matcha was seen as a status symbol as it was only produced in small quantities.

Popularity

Global Phenomenon

Matcha became popular in the West due to the influence of social media, which highlighted its "superfood benefits" and aesthetic appeal. It quickly became a trendy drink and caused an increased interest in Asian cultures. The matcha market in the US is estimated to exceed a value of 3 billion USD by 2033.

Versatility

Matcha is remarkably versatile, despite its distinct flavor. Its finely ground powder blends smoothly into cold and hot beverages, ranging from lattes to smoothies. Matcha is also a favorite flavor in culinary creations as it's slightly sweet flavor enhances goods such as cakes and cookies. Its adaptability, has helped it become a staple in kitchens worldwide.
